# Life Story Interview Questionnaire
*A comprehensive guide for capturing a life's journey in depth*

---

## Part I: Origins & Early Years

### Childhood Memories
- What is your earliest clear memory, and what details do you remember most vividly?
- How would you describe the neighborhood or environment where you grew up?
- What were some of your favorite activities or hobbies as a child?
- Were there any objects, places, or traditions that felt especially meaningful to you during childhood?
- How did you spend your summers or school breaks?
- Did you have any imaginary friends, secret hiding spots, or childhood rituals?
- What kind of child were you—quiet and observant, or bold and adventurous?

### Formative Experiences
- Were there any events from your childhood that shaped who you became?
- How did you learn about the world beyond your immediate surroundings?
- What were some of the rules or expectations in your household?
- Did you have any childhood heroes or role models?
- What were you afraid of as a child, and do any of those fears persist today?

---

## Part II: Family & Roots

### Parents & Guardians
- What do you know about your parents' lives before you were born?
- How would you describe each of your parents' personalities?
- What were your parents' values, and how did they express them?
- What do you remember most about your relationship with each parent?
- Were there any lessons or phrases from your parents that have stayed with you?

### Siblings & Extended Family
- What was it like growing up with your siblings (if applicable)?
- How did the dynamics between siblings shift as you all grew older?
- Which extended family members played a significant role in your life?
- Are there any family stories or legends that get retold at gatherings?
- How has your relationship with your family evolved over the years?

### Family Influence
- What traditions did your family maintain, and which ones do you still practice?
- Were there any secrets or unspoken rules in your family that you only understood later?
- How did your family handle conflict, celebration, or hardship?
- What do you wish you had asked your elders before they passed, if applicable?
- How has your family background shaped your worldview?

---

## Part III: Education & Learning

### Early Education
- What was your experience like in elementary/middle school?
- Were there any teachers who particularly influenced or inspired you?
- What subjects came easily to you, and which challenged you?
- How did you relate to your classmates and peers?
- Looking back, what would you have done differently in those years?

### Higher Education & Self-Directed Learning
- Did you pursue formal higher education, or take a different path?
- What drew you to your field of study (or self-directed learning)?
- Were there any pivotal educational moments that changed your direction?
- How did your education shape your identity and aspirations?
- What skills or knowledge have you pursued outside of formal schooling?

### Learning Philosophy
- How do you approach learning new things today?
- Are there subjects or skills you wish you had explored but haven't yet?
- What role has curiosity played throughout your life?
- How has education—both formal and informal—impacted your career and relationships?

---

## Part IV: Work & Vocation

### Career Journey
- What was your first job, and what did it teach you?
- How did you choose your career path, or did it choose you?
- What have been the most meaningful aspects of your work?
- Were there any career crossroads where you made a pivotal decision?
- How has your relationship with work evolved over the decades?

### Work Ethic & Meaning
- What does a "good day's work" mean to you?
- How do you define success in your professional life?
- What were the greatest challenges you faced in your career?
- Are there any projects or contributions you're most proud of?
- How do you balance work with other areas of life?

### Retirement & Legacy
- How did you approach the transition out of full-time work, if applicable?
- What do you miss about your working years, if anything?
- How do you want to be remembered by colleagues and in your profession?
- What advice would you give to someone starting in your field?

---

## Part V: Core Values & Beliefs

### Personal Ethics
- What values feel most essential to who you are?
- Was there a moment when your values were tested or clarified?
- How do you navigate situations where your values conflict with others'?
- Have your core values changed significantly over time?
- What role does integrity play in your daily life?

### Spiritual & Philosophical Foundations
- How would you describe your spiritual or religious journey, if any?
- What practices or rituals sustain you, whether religious or secular?
- How do you find meaning in everyday life?
- What questions about existence or purpose remain with you?
- How has your worldview deepened or shifted as you've aged?

### Guiding Principles
- Is there a motto, quote, or philosophy that guides you?
- What lessons have been hardest for you to learn?
- How do you approach forgiveness—both of others and yourself?
- What does living a "good life" mean to you?
- How do you want to behave when no one is watching?

---

## Part VI: Pivotal Moments

### Turning Points
- What would you consider the single most important turning point in your life?
- Were there any seemingly small decisions that led to major life changes?
- How did you process major transitions—move, loss, divorce, new beginning?
- What risks have you taken, and how did they turn out?
- Is there a moment you would relive if you could?

### Challenges & Adversity
- What has been the greatest challenge you've faced?
- How did you cope during the darkest periods of your life?
- Who or what helped you through difficult times?
- What strength did you discover in yourself that you didn't know existed?
- How have hardships shaped your perspective on life?

### Moments of Joy & Wonder
- What are among the happiest moments of your life?
- When have you felt most alive, most yourself?
- What experiences have filled you with awe or wonder?
- Are there any moments of pure, unexpected joy you still carry with you?
- What simple pleasures still bring you happiness?

---

## Part VII: Relationships & Love

### Romantic Life
- How did you meet your most significant romantic partner(s)?
- What do you remember about falling in love?
- What makes a relationship work, in your experience?
- What have been the hardest lessons in love?
- How have your ideas about love and partnership evolved?

### Friendship
- What makes a friend dear to you?
- How have your friendships changed over the years?
- Are there friendships from childhood or young adulthood that have lasted?
- What do you look for in a friend, and what do you strive to offer?
- How do you maintain connections as life becomes busier?

### Family Relationships
- How has becoming a parent (if applicable) changed you?
- What do you hope your children or descendants will remember about you?
- How do you define family beyond blood relations?
- What have you learned from the younger generations?
- How do you wish you had been a better family member?

---

## Part VIII: Achievements & Contributions

### Personal Milestones
- What achievements are you most proud of, big or small?
- Were there any accomplishments that surprised even you?
- What goals did you set that you never achieved, and how did you make peace with that?
- Which of your achievements felt most meaningful, and why?
- What are you working toward now?

### Contributions to Others
- How have you tried to make the world better, in big ways or small?
- What acts of service or generosity have been most fulfilling?
- Who have you mentored, helped, or championed?
- What legacy do you hope to leave behind?
- How do you want to be remembered by those you've touched?

### Unrecognized Aspects
- What strengths or accomplishments do others rarely see?
- Are there any contributions you're proud of that went unnoticed?
- What aspects of yourself have you underappreciated?
- How do you balance humility with self-recognition?

---

## Part IX: Regrets, Losses & What Remains

### Losses
- Who or what have you lost that still affects you?
- How did you grieve, and how long did it take?
- Were there any losses that taught you something profound?
- How do you keep the memory of lost loved ones alive?
- What rituals or practices help you process grief?

### Regrets
- What do you most wish you had done differently?
- Are there words you wish you had said—or hadn't said?
- What opportunities do you regret passing up?
- How do you make peace with regret?
- Is there anything you would apologize for, if you could?

### Acceptance
- What have you learned to accept about yourself or your life?
- How do you define closure, and have you found it?
- What resentments have you released, and which linger?
- How do you balance self-compassion with accountability?
- What remains unresolved that you still carry?

---

## Part X: The Present & The Future

### Current Life
- How would you describe your life right now?
- What brings you the most satisfaction in this chapter?
- What are the biggest challenges you face today?
- How has your daily routine evolved, and what does a typical day look like?
- What are you most grateful for in this moment?

### Looking Ahead
- What do you still want to experience or accomplish?
- Are there relationships you want to reconnect with or repair?
- What fears do you have about aging or the future?
- What gives you hope for the years ahead?
- If you could advise your younger self, what would you say?

### Final Reflections
- If you could write a letter to your future self, what would it say?
- What would you like your epitaph or memorial words to convey?
- What is the most important message you want to leave behind?
- If your life were a book, what would you want the final chapter to say?
- Is there anything else you'd like to share that we haven't covered?

---

*This questionnaire is designed to be used over multiple sessions, allowing time for reflection and the organic emergence of stories. Trust the conversation to guide you—some of the most profound answers come from unexpected tangents.*
